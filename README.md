# SQL Data Analysis Project

## Overview
In this project, I explored a customer dataset (`flo_data_20K`) to better understand purchasing behavior and key business metrics.  
The analysis focuses on identifying patterns in revenue, order frequency, and customer activity.

## Dataset
- Source: flo_data_20K  
- Contains customer-level order and revenue data  

## Approach
I started with basic exploration (row counts, unique customers), then moved into deeper analysis to answer key business questions such as:
- How much revenue is generated overall?
- What is the average revenue per order?
- Which channels and store types perform better?
- Who are the most valuable customers?

## Key Analyses

- **Total Orders and Total Revenue**  
  Overall performance of the business.

- **Average Revenue per Order**  
  Helps understand customer spending behavior.

- **Revenue and Order Volume by Last Order Channel**  
  Compares performance across channels.

- **Revenue and Order Volume by Last Order Channel**
  Analyzes how different sales channels perform in terms of total revenue and number of orders, helping to identify the most effective channel.

- **Total Revenue by Store Type**  
  Identifies which store types generate more revenue.

- **Most Preferred Store Type**  
  Indicates customer channel preference.

- **Order Volume by Customer Acquisition Year**  
  Shows how customer cohorts behave over time.

- **Top 10 Most Frequent Category Combinations (Last 12 Months)**  
  Highlights common purchasing patterns.

- **Customer with the Highest Order Volume**  
  Identifies highly active customers.

- **Purchase Frequency of Top 100 Customers by Revenue**  
  Focuses on high-value customer behavior.

- **Customers with the Most Recent Purchase Date**  
  Shows currently active users.

## Notes
This project is focused on SQL querying and analytical thinking.  
All queries used in the analysis can be found in the `analysis_queries.sql` file.
